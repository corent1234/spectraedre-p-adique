----- TEXT:
This paper discusses linear programming and semi-definite programs over valued fields. Over a valued field a semi-algebraic set has the form {x: val(p_i(x)) geq 0, 1 leq i leq m, p_j(x)=0, 1 leq j leq n}. The definitions for polyhedra and spectrahedra can be similarly modified (a polyhedron is {x : val(l_i(x)) geq 0, val(m_i(x))=infty} where the l_i, m_i are linear). The main results are that the image of a polyhedron under an affine map is a polyhedron, and that annuli of a field K with infinite residue field are semidefinite-representable but not spectrahedra.

These topics are very interesting, and the results are much simpler I would have expected.

The discussion of polyhedra is rather complicated, however. The fact that the image of a semialgebraic set is semialgebraic follows from the model theory of algebraically closed valued fields. I would have liked to have seen discussion of whether this result does as well. There is still significant value in an explicit proof, but it would be good to be clear as to what the value of this proof is.

The results on linear programming are surprising in their simplicity. Similarly, it is surprising how simple it is to distinguish concepts related to spectrahedra, unlike in the real case.

The paper is written in a straight-forward fashion, and should be of interest to the ISSAC audience. The results are interesting, but the presentation could be improved.

This paper extends notions about linear and semidefinite representability of convex sets from the real numbers to more general (discretely) valued fields, including algorithms in the linear case. This is a very natural line of research and makes the paper, in my opinion, a very good contribution to ISSAC. A more detailed review will follow.
